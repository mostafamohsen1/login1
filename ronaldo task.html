Cristiano Ronaldo

Biography

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro is a Portuguese footballer who plays as a forward for Serie A club Juventus and captains the Portugal national team. Often considered the best player in the world and one of the greatest of all time, Ronaldo has won five Ballon d'Or awards and four European Golden Shoes, the most for both awards by a European player. He holds the records for most international goals scored by a male player (117), most international goals scored for a European national team (117), most goals (140) in the UEFA Champions League, most goals (81) in the UEFA European Championship, most hat-tricks in the UEFA Champions League (8), and most hat-tricks in Serie A (3). He is also the only player to have scored in five consecutive UEFA World Cups.

Ronaldo began his career with Sporting CP, making his senior debut in 2002 at the age of 16. He moved to Manchester United in 2003, where he won three Premier League titles, two FA Cups, two League Cups, the UEFA Champions League, and the FIFA Club World Cup. In 2009, he was the subject of the most expensive transfer of all time when he moved from Manchester United to Real Madrid for €94 million. At Real Madrid, he won two La Liga titles, two Copa del Rey, two Supercopa de España, four UEFA Champions Leagues, three UEFA Super Cups, and two FIFA Club World Cups. In 2018, he moved to Juventus, where he has won two Serie A titles, two Supercoppa Italiana, and one Coppa Italia.

Ronaldo is Portugal's all-time leading goalscorer and has helped the country win the 2016 European Championship. He is the only player to have scored in five consecutive World Cups.
